How it works

The key fob is only powered when it needs to be.

Lo-Key contains a low-power motion sensor, controller, and electronic switch inside a coin-battery format.

01
When the key is in useLo-Key powers the fob normally, allowing passive entry, button presses, and vehicle start.
02
When the key is left stillAfter the active period expires without qualifying motion, Lo-Key disconnects the fob from the battery output.
03
When you pick it up againThe motion sensor wakes the power output automatically. No button, app, or paired device is required.
04
While the key spends time asleepThe key fob is not consuming its normal idle power, which may extend service life. Results depend on the key and the owner’s routine.

Compatibility

Start with the battery marking inside your key.

Lo-Key is intended for key fobs that currently use a CR2032 coin cell. Even within that category, compartment depth, contact geometry, and the fob’s pulse-current demand can differ.

  • 1Open the key fob and confirm the existing battery is marked CR2032.
  • 2Check that the battery compartment and contacts are not damaged or corroded.
  • 3Confirm your exact make, model, year, and key-fob style against the production compatibility list before ordering.

Pilot note: broad compatibility has not yet been claimed. The storefront wording is deliberately conservative until vehicle-by-vehicle validation is complete.

Safety & limitations

One layer of security—not a promise that a vehicle cannot be stolen.

Important product information

Lo-Key is designed to reduce exposure to relay attacks by removing power from an idle key fob. It does not stop theft methods that do not depend on the key’s wireless response.

Coin batteries can cause severe injury if swallowed. Keep the product and any removed battery away from children. Do not recharge, crush, puncture, short-circuit, disassemble, or expose it to fire or excessive heat.

Prototype units should be used only by informed adult testers. Final retail safety instructions, child-resistant packaging, transport compliance, and product certifications remain part of the production process.
