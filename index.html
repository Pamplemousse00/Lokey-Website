Relay attacks, simply explained

A thief can’t relay a signal that isn’t there.

In a relay attack, equipment near your home carries your key’s wireless response to the vehicle. The vehicle can be fooled into thinking the key is nearby—even when it is inside.

Lo-Key addresses one link in that chain: when the key has been still, it cuts power to the fob. With the fob off, there is no powered key signal to extend.

Three simple steps

Install it once.
Then forget about it.

Lo-Key moves the protection into something your key already needs: its battery.

1

Replace

Install Lo-Key in place of the key fob’s standard CR2032 battery.

No wiring. No pairing.
2

Sleep

After the key remains still, Lo-Key disconnects power from the fob while its motion sensor keeps watch.

The key stops answering relay equipment.
3

Wake

Pick up the key and motion wakes the fob automatically. A fresh active window begins.

Your normal routine stays normal.
